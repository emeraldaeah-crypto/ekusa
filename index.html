NYX

Born from the first night, this work opens like a field recording of darkness: thin gold syntax, slow signals, and a blue undercurrent moving behind the surface. It is not a single image so much as a suspended interval, a coded altar where distance keeps folding back into itself and the viewer feels the room become wider than the screen. The painting is meant to be watched slowly, as if the black field were breathing through layers of mineral dust, old monitor light, and a private astronomical notation.

CHAOS

Chaos is treated here as the original space before names, before edges, before a stable horizon. The painting holds still while the layers behind it move in different directions, creating the sense that a fluorescent green fault line is opening under the stars and the image is only temporarily balanced above it. The long text belongs to the work like a console report from a place that has not finished becoming a place yet.

OSIRIS

A reconstruction myth, a body of light assembled from fragments, panels, and false coordinates. The work appears as a ceremonial print drifting through black space, with orange heat at the edges and a grave gold register in the center, priced as an impossible object rather than a simple edition. Its surface suggests something recovered from a river of data: damaged, sacred, overvalued, and still carrying a pulse.

HERMES

Hermes carries the message sideways, never straight, slipping between the artwork, the interface, and the viewer's hand. The text behaves like a transmission log: mostly gold, occasionally interrupted by electric blue notation, while the flames below keep appearing and vanishing like packets of heat in a game world. It is a messenger piece, but the message is delayed on purpose, stretched into a corridor where reading and scrolling become the same ritual.

ISIS

Isis gathers broken color into a protective field. The image is quiet, but the surrounding space is not: small parallax layers pull in vertical and horizontal directions at once, and a green pulse appears like a hidden restoration spell written in programming font. The longer the viewer stays with it, the more the painting feels less like an object and more like an instruction for holding fragments together without fully repairing them.

HELIOS

Helios is the eye of scale. At one hundred percent it reads as a painting, but at one percent it becomes a distant artifact, almost lost inside the field; at extreme magnification it turns into texture, grain, and coded weather. The gold typography stays calm while solar orange leaks through the abstract surface. This work is about distance as a material: the small version is not a thumbnail, it is a faraway body.

ANUBIS

Anubis marks no ending. The sequence loops both left and right, sending each painting past the viewer as if weighed, archived, and released again into the same infinite corridor. The background parallax moves like a dark river, and the gold text returns with small flashes of blue, acid green, and ember orange. The work refuses a final door; it keeps the viewer inside a measured passage, where every image has already passed once and is still arriving.
